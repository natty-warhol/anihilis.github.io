Using Solus

I’ve been working on the Solus operating system (base kernel of Linux) for several months now (recently returned to Linux and all relevant things along from using a Macbook for a minute), as a laptop system, as well as for my server (boy was that a mistake).
I’ve reached that point however, where everything is superbly streamlined, and everything works without error, ever. AND IT’S SO BORING. I’ve always had the issue of being bored of my tech when I’m not messing with it from the most basic levels. It’s really hard to deal with this need to fuck with everything nowadays especially, due to my hardware finding itself becoming somewhat mission critical.
No longer am I a student or someone that doesn’t have expectations. I am expected to do things now, which my technology is involved in.

I’m bringing this up, because I found the perfect install script for Arch Linux, and I just thought to myself, “How amazing would it be to have a small base install of Arch Linux, and then build all my front-end apps through flatpak! I could guarantee a perfect experience every time with that, and could squeeze every clock of my CPU with that setup as well. But…. I already have that with my current setup. I’m just antsy, and I wanna do the thing.
So, now I hunt for a small laptop I can fuck with, and eventually probably turn into a testbed server… or some other B.S. thing I can come up with.

  • I’m so salty by the way. On my walk home from work today, I walked past a stack of servers….

Definitely all of them except for two were stripped of their guts… I assume those two were just jank as shit as well, but the not knowing is slowly eating away at me tonight. I may not survive this…
