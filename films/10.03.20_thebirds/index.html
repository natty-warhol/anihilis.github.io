10.03 - The Birds

The Birds (1963)

“A wealthy San Francisco socialite pursues a potential boyfriend to a small Northern California town that slowly takes a turn for the bizarre when birds of all kinds suddenly begin to attack people”. Directed by Alfred Hitchcock, with a screenplay by Evan Hunter and based off a story by Daphne Du Maurier. Right off the bat, this movie gets an automatic pass from me.
I’ve been watching this film since I was a wee child, and every time I watch it, I still notice something new about it. This movie never fails to excite the senses either, with the unbearably (by today’s standards) slow introduction credits over dubbed with birds cawing all encompassing, for instance.
Onward into the film, it really does one good by showing through not-awkward dialogue, and “showing instead of telling”. The main lady Tippi Hedren, being exposed as kind of a happy-go-lucky character, breaking laws and lacking cares because of her priviliged upbringing and lifestyle. Being exposed by the supported main man, Mitch Brenner (Rod Taylor), who is a character we learn about throughout the movie, as a caring older brother who has kind of become a father figure in the household since the loss of his father.
This movie does really well with what is has to offer, as well as what with the technological limitations of the time. A million out of a million for this bad boy. Hard recommend if you haven’t seen this one yet.
