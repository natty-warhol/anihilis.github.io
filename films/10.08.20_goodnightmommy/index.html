10.08 - Goodnight Mommy

Goodnight Mommy (2014)

Damn the Austrians know how to have a good time. “Twin boys move to a new home with their mother after she has face changing cosmetic surgery, but under her bandages is someone the children don’t recognize.”
Starring Lukas and Elias Schwarz as themselves, and Susanne Wuest as Mutter (German for Mother). The movie is in german, and it’s based in rural Austria, with some of the most warming visuals of the country. It’s seemgly fitting considering all that is sinister in this movie.

This movie leaves me speachless, in all the right ways. I heavily recommend this to anyone that can handle it. There is a bunch of detail gore (so not a lot of blood or anything, but like, lips getting glued together and shit like that), as well as high intensity moments for sure. It’s a very beautiful and heartbreaking movie, especially onces it’s over and you finally realize exactly what’s going on.
A lot of reviewers refer to this as something very Hitchcock-ian, and I agree whole-heartedly. This movie subverts all your expectations and does it without breaking any rules or making it obvious they’re hiding something from the plot. It’s the best surprise I’ve experienced in years.
100% though, I never get scared or uncomfortable from scary movies anymore it seems, but this movie had me on edge, and excited to see more.
Thank you Severin Fiala and Veronika Franz for making such a deliciously thick and well orchestrated inveritable panic attack.
