10.12 - The Void

The Void (2016)

“Shortly after delivering a patient to an understaffed hospital, a police officer experiences strange and violent occurences seemingly linked to a group of mysterious hooded figures.” - IMDb

Uhuh.

Well shit, buddy, If you’re looking for a good old raucus time of, “I don’t know what’s going on, but I don’t really care because look, pretty lights!”, then this is the movie for you. It’s thematically based off of lovecraftian stuff, and it definitely fits the bill almost perfectly. It has the evil cult, with the weird eugenics themes, trying to open some weird dimensional portal. That’s really it though. The main character has some stuff, and some weird purpose in the end (to become the cult leader’s weird dog-slave); though none of it really matters in the end of the movie really.

Oh hey! I totally forgot, the movie was directed by the duo of Jeremy Gillespie, and Steven Kostanski. Also, starring Aaron Poole (Daniel Carter, Main Character, Police Officer), and Kenneth Welsh (Dr. Richard Powell, the dude with the fucked up face, Main Antagonist).

I’m giving everything away in this movie, because it’s not really that great at keeping any good secrets, or giving any real hints either, this is very much a shut your mind off and stare kind of movie.

That’s not to say that it isn’t a great movie! It really is pretty good! It’s not revolutionary or life-changing, but it doesn’t have to be! It has a bit of camp, and it gets a little slow at times, and the ending isn’t the biggest surprise, but it’s pretty!

I don’t know man, I’m really trying here.
